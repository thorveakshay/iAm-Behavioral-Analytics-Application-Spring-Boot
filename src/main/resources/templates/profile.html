So What’s the Verdict????


Harmonizer
Values acceptance and stability in circumstances
Slow with big decisions; dislikes change
Builds networks of friends to help do work
Good listener; timid about voicing contrary opinions; concerned for others’ feelings
Easy-going; likes slow, steady pace
Friendly & sensitive; no person in unlovable
Relationship Oriented

Simply put, the tortoise wants everyone to get along. They are naturally empathetic, supportive, and care for others. In a group, they tend to be the “peacekeepers” or mediators. While they might not be directly involved in implementing a project, they are usually encouraging others to keep morale high. Tortoises have a hard time saying “no” and can find themselves stressed with the extra work they did not really want. While they see themselves as self-sacrificing, others may perceive this as laziness when they do not do the same style or amount of work as other teammates. Also, because of the sociality of tortoises, they tend to be talkative which can cause their efficiency to suffer.

Idea Oriented

Owls like to overlook their projects from a higher position that enables them to absorb the “larger picture.” They tend to be logical and analytical which allows them to be thorough in their work. They tend to do work alone in part because of their tendency to be perfectionists and their great attention to detail. They tend to be a little strict or inflexible with others because they need a project done a certain way.

Rabbits are the energetic go-getters. They often initiate the actions that get the process going and get the rest of the team on the process. They are usually enthusiastic and flexible in both their actions and interests and therefore function with little structure. Because of this bouncing back and forth, they may not take as much time as necessary on a project or fail to satisfactorily complete their work.

You are {{behaviour}}, and our magical behavior analytics says you are a Bear




So What’s the Verdict????


Commander
Values getting the job done
Decisive risk taker
Good at delegating work to others
Not shy but private about personal matters; comes on strong in conversation
Likes to be where the action is
Take charge, enterprising, competitive, efficient approach
Fearless; no obstacle is too big to tackle
Results Oriented

Bears are assertive yet supportive. In a group, they usually fall into the role of the leader. They are constantly taking action to keep the group going on task, but they are also quick to lend support to their teammates. Of the communication styles, the bears are the most aggressive and because of this they often grow impatient or angry when things don’t go their way. Their inflexibility and assertive attitudes can cause some issues within groups, but they are usually the driving forces to finish the project on time.
